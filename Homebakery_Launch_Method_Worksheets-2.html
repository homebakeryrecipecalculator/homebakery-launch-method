Instagram Profile Checklist
Lesson 5.3 · Instagram Basics
📸
Instagram is a long game — brand builder, not primary sales driver at launch. Set it up right, show up consistently, let it grow.
Profile Setup
  • Username close to your bakery name — simple and memorable
  • Profile photo — a real photo of YOU, same as Facebook if possible
  • Bio written — who + what + how to order, under 150 characters
  • Order link in bio (Simply Bread/Hotplate/Bakesy)
  • Switched to Creator or Business account (free — gives you insights)
Content — 3 Types, 3 Posts Per Week
Type 1 — Product
  • Natural light
  • Clean background
  • Close-up texture/detail
  • Order link in caption or "link in bio"
Type 2 — Process
  • Kitchen behind the scenes
  • Dough, shaping, oven, packaging
  • Short Reel if comfortable
  • Real and authentic — not staged
Type 3 — Person
  • Photo of you
  • Your story / your why
  • Faith, family, community
  • Real voice, honest words
Hashtag Strategy — 5 to 10 Per Post

Section 2 — Weekly Business SOP
Menu Day — Day:
  • Menu released on platform at
  • Text notification confirmed sent to all customers
  • Menu image posted to Facebook bakery page
  • Menu posted to local Facebook groups
  • Menu shared to personal Facebook page
  • Instagram post published — order link in bio updated
  • Comments and DMs checked and responded to
Prep Day — Day:
  • Ingredient inventory checked — anything needed noted
  • Grocery or supply run completed
  • Starter fed and maintained
  • Advance prep done — butter tempered, dry ingredients measured
  • Order list reviewed — totals, special requests, quantities
  • Labels printed if needed
Cutoff — Night before bake day at:
  • Orders officially closed at set cutoff time
  • Cutoff reminder sent — automated through platform
  • Final order list reviewed and locked
  • Production quantities finalized for bake day
Delivery Day — Day:
  • Pickup/delivery notifications sent to all customers
  • Store deliveries completed
  • Individual orders fulfilled
  • Profit First allocation done — revenue allocated to all 4 accounts
  • First-time customer follow-up messages sent
  • Cycle reflection noted — what worked, what to change
